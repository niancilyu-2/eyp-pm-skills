# Problem statement template

User-centered, root-cause-deep, evidence-grounded — and **no solution** inside it.

## One-line summary (lead with this)
> [One sentence: the user, the unmet outcome, and the cost of the gap.]

## Framing
**I am** [persona — 2–3 sourced traits] · **trying to** [the outcome they actually want] ·
**but** [the barrier today] · **because** [root cause, from 5-whys; label [Inference] if reasoned] ·
**which makes me feel** [stakes — tie to $ARR / churn where evidenced]. `[cite each clause]`

## Impact & timing (one or two lines, when the evidence supports it)
[Who / how many / magnitude — e.g. "7 of 12 accounts, $1.4M ARR" `source`.] [**Why now:** what
changed that makes this urgent.] If the data doesn't support these, say so rather than inventing.

## Success criteria — how we'll know it's solved
Problem-level outcomes (not feature metrics or RICE). Include a **guardrail** so a fix can't win one
number by wrecking another. Missing a baseline? Mark it `[Assumption]` or an open question — don't invent.

| Metric | Baseline | Target | Timeline |
|--------|----------|--------|----------|
| [primary outcome] | [value `source`] | [target] | [by when] |
| [guardrail — keep from regressing] | [value] | [maintain] | [ongoing] |

## Quality bar
- [ ] States the "what", not the "how" (no solution smuggled in).
- [ ] Specific persona/segment, not "all users"; every clause cited or labeled.
- [ ] Root cause, not a restated complaint.
- [ ] Success criteria have a baseline + target (or the gap is flagged) and a guardrail.

## Anti-patterns
- **Solution smuggling** — "Users need an A/B button." (That's a solution.)
- **Generic persona / symptom-as-cause** — "Enterprise users want more"; "pages load slowly."
- **Unmeasurable success** — "Improve the experience." (No baseline, target, or guardrail.)
