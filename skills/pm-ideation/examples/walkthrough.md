# pm-ideation — step-by-step walkthrough

The shape of a good run, one example per step. **Structural only** — derive names, paths, themes,
and every number from the actual inputs of your run; never copy citations or figures from
examples.

### Step 0 — Pre-flight: inventory
> List every input found, with formats, before reading any. Name anything referenced but missing —
> report it as missing rather than working around it silently.

### Step 1 — Ingest every signal
> Parse each file with the format helpers (computed, not eyeballed). Sum dollar figures from rows,
> not prose. Check for duplicate/child CRM rows before summing — flag and exclude them with the
> math shown.

### Step 2 — Synthesize themes (cluster, don't keyword-match)
> Related sub-themes merge into one theme when they describe the same underlying job failing.
> A theme table shows: legs of evidence per theme, VALIDATED (≥2 independent sources) vs. WEAK,
> and the counter-signal column — who *didn't* ask for it.
> Expect traps: a loud ask that is **already on the roadmap** (check before recommending), and a
> high-dollar account whose issue belongs to a **different buying center**.

### Step 3 — Competitive & roadmap gap
> Rate competitor capabilities Full/Partial/None/**Unknown** (never infer a None). Cross-reference
> the internal roadmap and cite the slide/section that shows the omission — an absence is a claim
> and needs a citation like any other.

### Step 4 — Frame the problem
> One line, user-centered, cause not symptom: who is blocked, doing what, with what consequence.
> Success criteria carry numbers and a guardrail; unmeasured baselines get an [Assumption] label.

### Step 5 — Recommendation (scored + challenged)
> RICE with sourced inputs and visible math; Impact argued customer-value-first with the
> commercial figures as evidence. Now/Next/Later placement names what it displaces. The red-team
> section states the strongest objection — and sometimes wins and reshapes the recommendation.
> Close with "what to validate next": the evidence gap that would most change the answer.
